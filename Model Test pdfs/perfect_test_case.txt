BRIEF FACTS
This is an appeal directed against the judgment of the Trial Court where the appellant was convicted. The prosecution alleged that the appellant engaged in a criminal conspiracy and committed murder. Consequently, the appellant was charged under Section 302 of the IPC and Section 120B of the Indian Penal Code. The trial court relied heavily on circumstantial evidence to declare the accused guilty and sentenced him to life imprisonment. 

SUBMISSIONS
The learned counsel for the appellant argued that the entire prosecution case is based on fabricated evidence. It was submitted that there is no direct eyewitness to the alleged murder. Furthermore, the defense pointed out that the charges of cheating under Section 420 of the IPC were added maliciously by the police to deny bail. The defense firmly states that the appellant is innocent and the FIR is entirely baseless.

COURT OBSERVATIONS
We have carefully examined the evidence on record and the testimonies of the witnesses. The prosecution has miserably failed to prove the chain of circumstances beyond a reasonable doubt. The trial court committed a grave error in ignoring the forensic reports which clearly exonerate the accused. The fundamental principle of criminal jurisprudence is that guilt must be proved beyond reasonable doubt. 

FINAL JUDGMENT
In light of the aforesaid reasons, the appeal is allowed. The impugned judgment of the trial court is set aside. The appellant is acquitted of all charges and shall be released forthwith.
